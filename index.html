Please Read Before Using

  • **This will not be 100% accurate for some brands, please do reseach & be extra careful when checking for Manufacture Date**
  • This website works best with Dr.Pepper.
  • Manufacture Date (MD) also called Sell-By-Date (SBD)
  • Some codes can also serve as the Expiration Date.
  • Many manufacturers use different SBD formats.
  • Date formats - Example:
    1. MM DDD Y
    2. MMM DD YY
    3. M Y DDD
    4. YYY MM
    5. DDD YY
    6. M YY DD
    7. Some will look like this: L8024820 -> 24820 this is called Julian Date which 248 is for the date and 20 is for the year -> September 4, 2020.
    8. Or like this for the Julian Date 23304 -> 2023 304 -> Oct 31st 2023.
    9. The Julian Date MUST always consist of 5 digits. -> 23001 -> 2023001 -> Jan 1st 2023.
    10. *I'll update for new date formats if I come across them*
  • Read Shelf Life Explanation carefully putting items in FOODGRADE Donation containers.
  • If you're in 2023 and the Dr.Pepper can has the number 0, it means it was made in 2020, not the year it will expired (2030)
  • If you see 15:54 this means the time (3:54pm)

Shelf Life Explanation

  • Read - USDA KNOWLEDGE ARTICLE
  • Each soda product will have a different shelf life (when opened, stored in a dry/cool area, or refrigerated)
  • Sodas can last for 3 to 9 months or longer when unopened.
  • For best quality, consume unopened diet sodas within 3 months after the date expires; regular sodas within 9 months.
  • Monster drinks have a shelf life of 2 years.
  • To determine the expiration date, add the estimated shelf life to the manufacturing date - 2 years + 2023 = 2025 or October + 3 months = January.
